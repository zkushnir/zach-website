About

Background, education, and skills.

I grew up in Syracuse, NY and first got hooked on engineering in high school through design-and-build courses where I could turn ideas into physical things. That curiosity — “how far can I push what I can design and build?” — eventually led me to Pittsburgh.

I completed my B.S. in Mechanical Engineering at the University of Pittsburgh, where I cycled between co-op rotations at Acutronic, research in soft and morphing materials, and teaching first-year engineering students. Those experiences narrowed my interests toward robotics and human–machine systems.

I’m now pursuing my M.S. in Mechanical Engineering at Carnegie Mellon University, specializing in robotics. In the NeuroMechatronics Lab, I design and prototype a hand exoskeleton aimed at restoring motor function for people after stroke and spinal cord injury, blending mechanics, actuation, and EMG-based control.

In parallel, I continue to train and compete in Olympic-style karate as a member of Team USA. The combination of high-level sport and research shapes how I work: structured, intense, and focused on long-term progress.
